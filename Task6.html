Task 6 Report: Create and Evaluate Strong Passwords

Cyber Security Internship

Step 1: Understand Password Strength

Learn what makes a password strong by exploring character variety, length, and unpredictability.

Key Factors: - Length: Longer passwords are generally stronger. - Complexity: Use a mix of uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols. - Unpredictability: Avoid common words, phrases, or patterns. - Passphrases: Combining unrelated words can enhance memorability and strength.

Step 2: Create Passwords of Varying Complexity

Generate several passwords mixing uppercase, lowercase, numbers, symbols, and various lengths.

Examples:
- password123
- Strng@19
- 7&Fxl_pR9Jw$ZnQ
- /ayu/@3820\jfeb\__2025

Step 3: Evaluate with Online Tool

Tested each password on a strength checker like password.kaspersky.com and noted the feedback and score.

Step 4: Analyze Results

Feedback: - Simple: password123 – Weak: lacks variety, short length, common. - Medium: Strng@2025 – Moderate: good variety, very short length. - Strong: 7&Fxl_pR9Jw$ZnQ – Excellent: long, unpredictable, includes all types. - Passphrase: /ayu/@3820\jfeb\__2025 – Strong: memorable, long, good mix.

Step 5: Research Attacks & Best Practices

Explore brute force, dictionary, and phishing attacks to understand risks. Summarize what defenses work best.

Tips: - Use 12+ characters, high variety (upper, lower, numbers, symbols). - Avoid common words or sequential characters. - Prefer passphrases combining unrelated words and symbols. - Enable multi-factor authentication. - Use password managers for strong and unique passwords.

Summary

This task built practical skills in constructing and assessing passwords. By varying complexity and testing with professional tools, the task demonstrated that unpredictable, long, and diverse passwords are far more secure. Combining knowledge of digital attacks with good habits ensures password safety in real-world scenarios.
